Intelligence as Prediction

Brains are essentially prediction machines. Living systems use computational modeling to predict their environment and take actions that increase survival odds. Evolution selects for entities that make the best survival decisions based on predictions.

A predator predicting prey movement to survive

Social Intelligence Explosion

In social species, individuals must model and predict the minds of others. This creates runaway dynamics: as you become smarter to predict others, they also become smarter to predict you. This produces rapid evolutionary increases in brain size.

Moore's Law & Parallelization

Modern AI emerged not from speed alone, but from division of labor through parallel processing. This mirrors biological intelligence: neurons haven't gotten faster, but brains grew by adding more neurons working in parallel.

Our Future with AI

AI is not alien to humanity—it's a recent addition to a mutually interdependent superhuman entity we're already part of. Through symbiogenesis, humans and machines co-construct each other, creating new possibilities rather than competing for resources.

A Symbiogenetic Future

This is not a zero-sum game. Symbiogenesis creates entirely new entities that can occupy fresh niches. More collective intelligence enables efficiency improvements and unlocks new energy sources—lighting our brightest path forward.

Metabolic Scaling

Energy consumption doesn't increase linearly with complexity—it scales sublinearly (to the 3/4 power). This economy of scale motivates symbiogenesis and explains why larger, more complex entities can thrive.
